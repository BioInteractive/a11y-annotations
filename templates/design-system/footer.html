Design System Footer

Footer Overview

The footer provides secondary navigation and organizational information that appears at the bottom of BioInteractive pages. It helps users locate supporting content, contact information, and important policy links.

The footer design remains consistent across the site to maintain familiarity and predictable navigation.

Footer Structure

The BioInteractive footer uses a dark background to visually separate it from the main page content. Links and icons are styled to maintain strong contrast and clear readability.

  • The BioInteractive logo reinforces brand identity.
  • Social media icons provide quick access to external channels.
  • Navigation links are grouped into logical categories.
  • Organization information and policy links appear at the bottom of the footer.

This structure allows users to quickly locate additional information without distracting from the primary page content.

Grouped Navigation Links

Footer links are organized into labeled groups to make secondary navigation easier to scan. Common groups include organization information and ways to connect with BioInteractive.

  • Organization links may include pages such as About Us, Our Research, Our Team, and Our Advisors.
  • Connect links may include resources such as FAQ, Contact Us, and Newsletter.

Grouping links helps users locate information quickly while maintaining a clean layout.

Responsive Behavior

The footer layout adapts for smaller screens by stacking content vertically. Navigation groups, icons, and organizational information are arranged in a single column to support readability and touch interaction.

This responsive structure ensures that footer content remains accessible and easy to navigate on mobile devices.

Accessibility Considerations

The footer should maintain strong color contrast between text and background to support readability. Links and icons must remain clearly identifiable and easy to interact with.

Consistent spacing and clear link labeling help ensure that users can navigate footer content efficiently across all devices.

Supporting Information

Some BioInteractive experiences include a supporting information panel that provides additional context about the interactive content. This panel typically includes information such as summaries, usage instructions, accessibility notes, credits, and terms of use.

Supporting information helps learners and educators understand how an interactive works and where the content originates. Links to this information are often provided through the footer or other persistent interface controls.

The panel should remain easy to access without interrupting the primary learning experience, allowing users to review supporting documentation when needed.
