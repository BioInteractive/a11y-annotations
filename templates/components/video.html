Ensuring video content is accessible is crucial for providing an inclusive user experience. Failure to make video components accessible can significantly impact users with disabilities, preventing them from accessing essential information and enjoying content. The following guidelines help developers build accessible video components, ensuring all users can interact with and benefit from video content effectively.

For comprehensive guidance and examples, refer to the WAI Media Accessibility User Requirements.

  • Color Contrast for Video Controls: Ensure all video controls (e.g., play, pause, volume) have sufficient color contrast against their background to meet the minimum contrast ratio requirement of 3:1. This enhances visibility for users with visual impairments.
  • Focus Indicators for Interactive Elements: Provide clear focus indicators for all interactive elements within the video component to assist keyboard users in navigating and interacting with the controls effectively.
  • Accessible Name for iframes: If the video component uses an iframe, ensure the iframe has an accessible name with the title attribute describing the video content. This helps screen reader users understand the content of the iframe.
  • Accessible Name for Custom Video Elements: If using a custom HTML video element, ensure it has an accessible name. The accessible name should describe the content of the video, aiding users who rely on assistive technologies.
  • Captions and Descriptive Transcripts: Ensure that captions are available for all video audio content. Consult with content leads to determine if descriptive transcripts or audio descriptions are also necessary and how they should be included in development.
  • Keyboard and Mouse Accessibility: Make all video controls mouse, touch, and keyboard accessible, ensuring all users can navigate and operate the video player equally. This may involve adding additional event handlers for keyboard accessibility.
  • Responsive Video Content: Ensure that all video content is responsive and works well on different screen sizes and devices, maintaining accessibility across platforms. This includes ensuring controls and content adjust appropriately for various device orientations and sizes.
