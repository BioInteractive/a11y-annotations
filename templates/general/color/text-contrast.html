Text Color Contrast

1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) requires that the visual presentation of text and images of text must have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and at least 3:1 for large text. Proper contrast ensures readability for users with low vision, color blindness, and other visual impairments.

Briefing and Intent

The goal of this success criterion is to ensure that all users, especially those with low vision, can read and understand on-screen text. Contrast minimum requirements help prevent text from blending into the background, making content more legible across different lighting conditions and display settings.

Key Requirements

  • Regular Text Contrast: Text smaller than 18pt (24px) must maintain a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 against the background.
  • Large Text Contrast: Large-scale text (18pt/24px and larger, or bold 14pt/18.5px) must have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1.
  • Incidental Text: Text that is purely decorative, part of an inactive interface component, or not intended to be read does not need to meet contrast requirements.
  • Logo Text: Text that is part of a logo or brand name does not have a contrast requirement.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using light gray text on a white background, which can cause readability issues.
  • Failing to account for different screen brightness settings or color blindness.
  • Assuming that bold text compensates for low contrast—bold only helps if it meets the large text threshold.

Ensuring Text Color Contrast

Begin by establishing an accessible color palette that adheres to contrast requirements for all text colors and background color combinations.

To meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA requirements, use the Color Contrast Analyzer tool by TPGI to assess and adjust the contrast of text against their background colors:

  1. Download the Tool: Obtain the Color Contrast Analyzer from TPGI's official website to help identify contrast ratios in your designs.
  2. Measure Foreground vs. Background: Use the tool's eyedropper feature to select the foreground (text) and background colors directly from your design.
  3. Analyze Contrast Ratios: The tool will automatically calculate the contrast ratio, allowing you to quickly assess conformance with WCAG standards.
  4. Adjust as Needed: For text under 18pt or 24px, aim for a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1. For large text (over 18pt or 24px, or bold and at least 14pt or 18.5px), ensure a ratio of at least 3:1.

Include contrast checks as a standard part of your design workflow to ensure accessibility for users with visual impairments.

Conducting Color Contrast Audit with Annotations

To ensure compliance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA color contrast requirements, auditors should use the Color Contrast Analyzer tool by TPGI during their review process:

  1. Access the Tool: Navigate to TPGI's official website and use the Color Contrast Analyzer to evaluate contrast ratios within Figma designs or web pages.
  2. Identify Text Size and Style: Determine if the text is regular or large sized based on its size and boldness to apply the correct contrast ratio requirement (4.5:1 for regular text, 3:1 for large text).
  3. Select Colors: Utilize the tool's eyedropper feature to pick the foreground (text) and background colors directly from the design or webpage.
  4. Calculate Contrast Ratio: The tool will automatically compute the contrast ratio between the selected foreground and background colors.
  5. Evaluate Compliance: Compare the calculated contrast ratio with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards to assess whether the design meets or fails the requirements.
  6. Recommend Adjustments: If the contrast ratio does not meet the minimum requirements, suggest specific color adjustments to the design team.
  7. Annotate Designs: Use the copy and paste annotations below to add notes to Figma boards to communicate requirements, failures and recommendations.

Implementing Color Contrast for Accessibility

When developing web content, ensuring sufficient color contrast is critical to comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards:

  • Text and Images of Text: Ensure regular-sized text and images of text have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 against their background. For large-sized text, a minimum ratio of 3:1 is required.
  • Color Contrast Tools: Utilize color contrast tools to verify the contrast ratio of text against the background colors during development.
  • Dynamic Content: For dynamic content that changes state (e.g., hover, focus), ensure contrast requirements are met in all states.
  • CSS Techniques: Apply CSS techniques to adjust text color, background color, and border styles to meet contrast ratios.
  • Automated Testing: Incorporate automated testing tools into your development workflow to identify and correct contrast issues.
  • Documentation: Document color usage and contrast ratios in your project's style guide to maintain consistency and compliance.

Adhering to these guidelines not only meets legal and regulatory requirements but also ensures your web content is accessible to users with visual impairments, including those with color vision deficiencies.
