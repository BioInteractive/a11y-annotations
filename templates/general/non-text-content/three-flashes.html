Three Flashes or Below

2.3.1 Three Flashes or Below Threshold Web pages must ensure that content does not flash more than three times per second to prevent seizures and reactions in susceptible users. This criterion aims to protect users from content that could cause serious health risks.

  • Flash Limits: Ensure no content flashes more than three times in any one-second period.
  • Below Threshold: If flashing is necessary, it must be below the general and red flash thresholds to minimize risks.
  • Non-Interference: All webpage content, regardless of its purpose, must adhere to this criterion to prevent interference with the user's ability to use the page fully.

Design Considerations for Three Flashes or Below Threshold

  • Coordinate with Content Leads: Work with content leads to identify any elements that may flash or have animations. Ensure these elements comply with the flash thresholds to prevent seizures.
  • Plan Mindful Transitions: When designing transitions or animations, ensure they do not flash more than three times per second. Use smooth transitions and avoid rapid flashing effects.
  • Review Animations Carefully: Critically assess any animations or video content within the design for flashing risks and adjust as necessary to meet the criteria.

By taking these steps during the design phase, designers can help ensure that digital content is safer and adheres to WCAG 2.3.1 criteria, protecting users from potential seizures caused by flashing content.

Auditing for Flashing Content Accessibility

  • Inspect for Flashing Elements: Review the design for any elements that may flash, including transitions and animations. Flag any components that could potentially flash more than three times in one second.
  • Video Content Review: Ensure any embedded or planned video content is assessed for flashing elements that exceed the thresholds. Videos may require a detailed review or consultation with content creators.
  • Consultation with Content Leads: Collaborate with content leads to verify if any dynamic content will include flashing or blinking elements. Document and communicate any concerns that may affect accessibility.
  • Recommendations for Mitigation: Provide actionable recommendations for redesigning or modifying elements that fail the flashing content criteria, including alternatives for transitions and animations that maintain the design's intent without causing seizures or physical reactions.

Conducting thorough audits for flashing content ensures designs adhere to WCAG 2.3.1 criteria, preventing seizures and providing a safer experience for all users.

Timing Adjustable

  • Content must not flash more than three times per second.
  • Warning: Ensure content does not flash more than three times per second.
  • Recommendation: Do not use flashing content, animations or videos that contain flashing.

Developing with Flashing Content Guidelines

  • Implement Safe Animations: Ensure animations and transitions do not flash more than three times per second. Use CSS or JavaScript libraries that adhere to safe animation practices.
  • Video Content Checks: For embedded videos, verify that they do not contain flashing elements above the thresholds. Utilize accessible video players that allow users to control playback.
  • Provide Control Mechanisms: Offer users the ability to stop, pause, or hide animations, especially for dynamic content that might flash.
  • Testing and Validation: Use automated testing tools and manual checks to validate that no content flashes more than three times per second, ensuring compliance with WCAG 2.3.1.

By following these development practices, you contribute to creating web content that is safe and accessible, preventing risks of seizures and complying with WCAG 2.3.1 Three Flashes or Below Threshold criteria.
