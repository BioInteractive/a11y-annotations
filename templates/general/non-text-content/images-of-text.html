Images of Text

1.4.5 Images of Text If the technologies being used can achieve the visual presentation, text is used to convey information rather than images of text. If an author can use text to achieve the same visual effect, he or she should present the information as text rather than using an image.

Exceptions include the following:

  • Customizable: When the image of text can be visually customized to the user's requirements;
  • Essential: A particular presentation of text is essential to the information being conveyed.
  • Logotypes (text that is part of a logo or brand name) are considered essential.

1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum): The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 (some exceptions). (Level AA)

As a Designer using Figma, your role in ensuring accessibility for images begins with thoughtful planning and documentation:

  • Choice of Images: You're free to choose images that best suit the design needs, but be mindful about the use of text within images.
  • Avoid embedding text directly into images to ensure the content is accessible to all users, including those using screen readers.
  • Only embed text directly into images when it is absolutly necessary to use a special font or its essentia to the information being conveyed.
  • Documentation: For each image you decide to include in the design, document its purpose and whether it's decorative or meaningful. This documentation will be invaluable for developers and content authors when they assign alternative text or decide on the appropriate ARIA attributes.
  • Consult with Content Leads: For complex or meaningful images, work closely with content leads or authors to understand the context and the message the image conveys. This collaboration ensures that appropriate and accurate alternative text can be developed, enhancing accessibility.

By incorporating these practices into your workflow, you contribute significantly to creating images and designs that are accessible and inclusive from the start.

Annotations for failing Images of Text

Image of Text

  • Image does not qualify as an exception.
  • Text must be real text content.
  • Text on image does not pass minimum contrast requirements.

Implementing Images for Accessibility

Proper implementation of text on top of images is crucial for web accessibility, adhering to WCAG 2.1 standards, particularly for non-text content and images of text:

  • Images of Text: When absolutely neccesary ensure that all text on image is included in the text alternative so it will be programmatically determinable.
  • Use HTML Text Instead of Images of Text: Avoid using images of text unless absolutely necessary. If images of text are used, ensure they meet contrast requirements and provide an accessible alternative for the text content.
  • Testing and Validation: Use accessibility testing tools to validate the presence of alt text and appropriate use of ARIA attributes for images, SVGs, and icons.

Following these guidelines ensures that all users, including those using assistive technologies, can fully engage with your web content.
