Accessibility Annotation Repository

The Accessibility Annotation repository empowers design auditors to collaborate with Figma designers and streamline the preparation of designs for developer handoff. Designers receive guidance from auditors, who identify potential accessibility issues and provide recommendations based on accessibility requirements, specifications, and best practices. Auditors ensure that designs adhere to accessibility specifications, WCAG requiremenets and consistently exhibit expected behaviors, facilitating a seamless transition for developers. Additionally, the repository offers valuable tips and guidelines, encouraging designers to consider accessibility from the early stages of the product lifecycle.

How to use this repository for designers and developers:

Designer

  • Review Annotations: Regularly check the repository for accessibility annotations related to current design projects.
  • Incorporate Feedback: Apply recommendations from auditors to address potential accessibility issues in your designs.
  • Consult Guidelines: Utilize the repository's guidelines and best practices to improve the accessibility of your designs.
  • Collaborate with Auditors: Engage with auditors through the repository for clarification or additional insights on accessibility concerns.
  • Iterate Designs: Continuously refine designs based on feedback to ensure adherence to WCAG requirements and accessibility specifications.
  • Focus on Accessibility Early: Leverage the repository to integrate accessibility considerations into the early stages of design.

Developer

  • Access Annotated Designs: Use the repository to view designs that have been audited for accessibility compliance.
  • Understand Accessibility Requirements: Familiarize yourself with the accessibility annotations that highlight specific WCAG requirements and recommendations.
  • Implement Accessible Features: Develop features and functionalities in line with the audited designs and accessibility guidelines.
  • Collaborate for Clarity: Collaborate with designers and auditors if there are uncertainties or need for clarification regarding the accessibility annotations.
  • Test and Validate: After implementing the designs, perform your own accessibility tests to ensure compliance.
  • Iterative Improvement: Continuously update your development based on ongoing feedback from the repository to refine accessibility features.

Understanding the Structure of Our Accessibility Repository

The intention of this Accessibility Annotation Repository, a dedicated space designed for enhancing web accessibility through structured and focused auditing. This repository categorizes essential accessibility elements into distinct sections, each addressing key aspects of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Our meticulously crafted categories include 'Structure', 'Color', 'Non-text Content', 'Interactives' and 'Screen Magnification'. These sections cover vital criteria and guidance, ensuring a comprehensive approach to accessibility.

The purpose of this categorization is to streamline the process of auditing Figma designs and developing accessible web content. It serves not only as a tool for pinpointing accessibility lapses but also as an educational resource to avert such issues in future projects. Categories are grouped and tailored to offer guidance in creating universally accessible designs and accessible web content. This repository is an indispensable resource for both designers and developers, bridging the gap between accessible design intention and practical implementation.
