1 Structures and Pages Users can make sense of the structure of the content on each page and understand how to operate and navigate within the system. Do the pages have meaningful title text, with page-specific information going first?
Do the pages provide at least two methods of locating a page on the site?
Is there a mechanism available to bypass blocks of content that are repeated on multiple pages?
Do the pages have meaningful headings for each major section?
Are data tables clearly assigned header columns and/or rows?
2 Structure - Forms Interactive controls have persistent, meaningful labels and instructions to help prevent mistakes, and provide users with clear error states which indicate what the problems are - and how to fix them whenever errors are returned. Are persistent, visible labels specified on all form controls?
Are labels and instructions displayed in close visual proximity to their controls?
Are required fields identified as such in the label text?
Are form instructions worded in text, to provide users with adequate support?
Are form errors indicated in ways that don’t rely on sensory cues alone?
Are inline error messages provided, with suggestions on how to fix them?
3 Color Text and other meaningful information can be easily distinguished and read by users of the system. Is information conveyed by means other than just color alone?
Is the foreground/background contrast ratio of regular sized text at least 4.5:1 (3:1 for large text)?
Are the links in text blocks clearly marked by a different color with at least a 3:1 contrast from the surrounding text, or are they indicated by a method that doesn't rely on color?
Does the contrast ratio between UI components/graphical objects and their adjacent background reach at least 3:1?
Does the border of essential interactive UI components have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 against the immediate surrounding color?
4 Navigation and Wayfinding Users can easily navigate, find content, and determine where they are at all times within the system. Is there a clear, visible indicator set on all active elements as they receive focus?
Does the page have meaningful title text, with page-specific information going first?
Are the page title element and H1 the same or similar?
Does the page have meaningful headings for each major section?
Can the links’ purpose be defined from link text alone, or their immediate context?
Is a "skip link" provided at the very top of the page, and is it revealed on focus?
Does the organization of navigational elements facilitate wayfinding?
5 Non-Text Content Purely visual or auditory content that conveys information has text-based alternatives for users who can't see or hear. Are informative images provided with meaningful alt text describing their content?
Are active images provided with meaningful alt text describing their purpose?
Are decorative images identified so they can be ignored by assistive technologies?
Are complex images given alt text and an extended full-text description?
Is a transcript placeholder designed for audio-only and video-only content?
Are synchronized captions provided for pre-recorded videos?
Are audio description tracks provided for pre-recorded videos?
6 Interactives Users can efficiently interact with the system using the input method of their choosing (i.e. mouse, keyboard, touch, etc.). Are any of the interactions designed to be mouse specific?
Is every functionality designed to be fully keyboard accessible?
Are features designed to be fully functional using touch screens?
Are the design interactions realistic from a tabbing, touch or voice perspective?
Can the design interactions be operated through voice commands?
Is the focus order structured to ensure a logical navigation sequence?
Has a visible focus indicator been provided for all interactive elements?
7 Movement and Flashing Elements on the page that move, flash, or animate in other ways can be stopped, and do not distract or harm the users. Can moving or animated content be paused, stopped, or hidden?
Can auto-updated content be fully controlled by the users?
Can the rate at which content is auto-updated be controlled by the user?
Are video and audio files not set to auto-play?
Is audio volume adjustable via a visible control?
Are there any flashing or blinking effects faster than 3 times per second?
Are users required to react quickly to information or user interface features?
8 Timing and Preservation Users are given enough time to complete tasks and do not lose information if their time (i.e., a session) runs out. Are users provided with a mechanism to ask for time extensions ahead of time?
Are upcoming session timeouts clearly identified as such in the design?
Can users turn off, adjust or extend time limits when sessions are about to run out?
Does the design offer options to postpone or suppress interruptions?
Can users request content updates, instead of content being updated automatically?
Is there a mechanism to allow data recovery after users re-authenticate?
Can users save data while filling out forms, so it can be used after re-authentication?
9 Screen Magnification Users are given the ability to resize text up to 200%, zoom and scale up to 400% without loss of content or functionality and/or required scrolling in 2 dimensions. Is zooming or scaling limited or restricted?
Are users able to resize text up to 200 percent without loss of content or functionality?
Do web designs accommodate text scaling while preserving content and functionality?
Are users able to resize text up to 400 percent without loss of content or functionality?
When users resize up to 400% is scrolling required in two dimensions?
Does all meaningful content remain available and not obscured or cut off due to resizing?
When text spacing is applied is there a loss of content or functionality?
